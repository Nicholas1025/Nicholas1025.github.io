# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, collaborators, lecturers, and technical peers visiting Nicholas Tay's personal portfolio. They are scanning for capability, taste, project depth, and personality in a short visit.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio presents Nicholas as an ML and backend engineer with a distinct visual voice. Success means the site feels memorable, technically capable, personal, and cohesive across Home, Me, Work, writing, and the future Eye photography page.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, technical, outdoors-minded. The voice should feel like a terminal log written by someone who also reads mountain routes: calm, precise, curious, and not corporate.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like a generic SaaS portfolio, a bright template grid, or a formal resume website. Avoid colorful gradient AI aesthetics, beige editorial defaults, ordinary card-heavy portfolio pages, and disconnected page themes.

## Design Principles

1. Treat the site as an explorable system interface, not a standard portfolio.
2. Keep the palette black and white, with texture, depth, motion, and typography carrying personality.
3. Let hiking, routes, field notes, and quiet terminal language shape the metaphors.
4. Prefer fewer stronger interface moments over many generic sections.
5. Make every page feel like it belongs to the same operating system.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep text contrast high on dark backgrounds, avoid mobile horizontal overflow, and keep decorative canvas effects pointer-events-free and nonessential.
